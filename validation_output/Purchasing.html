7. Explicit Risk Notes

The following risk notes highlight areas requiring immediate attention before RFP submission:

Unanswered Requirement (R16) — Contractual Exposure

Row 16 ('Need capabilities to determine which items generate variance / replacement') has no availability code. Per the RFP Instructions: 'Items that are not addressed by the Vendor\'s response will be assumed as not available or not supported.' This creates a direct contractual risk where the client may assume the solution cannot identify variance-generating items, despite D365 F&O having this capability through standard costing and variance reports.

Penalty Setup Overstated as Standard (R29)

Row 29 marks 'Vendor lead times and Penalty setup' as 'S' (Standard). While vendor lead times are indeed standard, penalty setup for late deliveries is not a D365 F&O standard feature. If the client relies on the 'S' classification to expect out-of-the-box penalty management, this creates a scope gap during implementation. The penalty portion should be clearly flagged as requiring customization.

Terms & Conditions Potentially Over-Classified (R22, R23)

Rows 22 and 23 mark PO Terms & Conditions and Printable T&C Library as 'M' (Modification). While customization may enhance the experience, basic T&C functionality is available through D365 F&O Print Management and Document Handling. Over-classifying as 'M' may inflate implementation cost estimates unnecessarily and could lead to scope disputes.

Cross-Functional Visibility Assumptions (R39, R68)

Rows 39 and 68 are marked 'S' but involve cross-functional visibility that may require workspace configuration or saved views beyond default setup. While technically achievable without code, the implementation effort for proper configuration should be planned.

Import Document Compliance Gap (R68)

Row 68 requires checking availability of all documents for imported items. While D365 F&O tracks documents, a systematic import compliance checklist is not standard. For organizations with significant import operations, this may require Quality Management configuration or a specialized trade compliance solution.
