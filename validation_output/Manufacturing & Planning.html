7. Explicit Risk Notes

  • 7 requirements have no availability code. Per the RFP Instructions tab, 'Items that are not addressed by the Vendor\'s response will be assumed as not available or not supported.' This creates significant contractual risk.
  • 3 items marked as 'S' (Standard) may be overstated. D365 F&O Production Control provides operations and scheduling but some described features (checklists, team leader assignments, specific dashboard formats) may require Asset Management module, workflow customization, or Power BI development.
  • The Factory Operations section (R4-R39) contains the highest concentration of risk. Many requirements describe specialized manufacturing workflows (checklists, team management, AutoCAD integration, area-based calculations) that go beyond standard Production Control.
  • MRP (R42-R49), Capacity Planning (R52-R59), and Routings (R104-R119) sections are well-answered and accurately reflect D365 F&O capabilities.
  • Manufacturing Orders section (R62-R101) is mostly well-answered but has critical gaps at R63 (outsourcing), R64 (status choices), and R69 (Autodesk estimation) that must be addressed before submission.

8. Verification Gaps

  • R22 (Small Pieces list track): Requirement is ambiguous ('Not Clear'). Cannot verify without client clarification on what constitutes 'small pieces' tracking vs. standard inventory item tracking.
  • R69 (Estimation linked to Autodesk): Complex requirement spanning standard estimation and external CAD integration. The Autodesk integration scope needs definition before a proper availability code can be assigned.
  • R36 (Check in/out with area/duration calculations): The specific calculation formulas (paint area per part, laser cut time per meter per thickness) cannot be verified as standard without examining D365 F&O route operation formula capabilities in detail.
  • R109 (Overhead prorate by project size): The exact mechanism for project-size-based overhead proration needs validation against D365 F&O costing sheet capabilities.
